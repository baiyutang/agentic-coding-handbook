# Further Reading

This handbook distills a fast-moving field. If you want to go deeper — or check our thinking
against the primary sources — start here. Links are external and may change.

## Reports & Research

- **Anthropic — _2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report_** — The data behind the "collaboration
  paradox," the shift from implementer to orchestrator, and productivity-through-output-volume.
  Grounds much of [What is Agentic Coding?](/getting-started/what-is-agentic-coding).
  [Report (PDF)](https://resources.anthropic.com/hubfs/2026%20Agentic%20Coding%20Trends%20Report.pdf)

## Official Guidance

- **Anthropic — Claude Code best practices** — Practical, first-party advice on `CLAUDE.md`,
  planning, and agentic workflows.
  [anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-best-practices](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-best-practices)
- **Claude Platform Docs** — Models, pricing, tool use, and the API.
  [platform.claude.com/docs](https://platform.claude.com/docs)
- **Model Context Protocol** — The open standard behind [MCP](/reference/hooks-and-mcp) tools.
  [modelcontextprotocol.io](https://modelcontextprotocol.io)

## Foundational Methodology & Thinkers

- **Kent Beck — _Test-Driven Development: By Example_** — The origin of the red-green-refactor
  loop our [TDD](/workflows/tdd) chapter builds on. The methodology predates AI but fits it
  perfectly.
- **Andrej Karpathy — "vibe coding"** — Karpathy popularized the term for the fast,
  by-feel mode we contrast with agentic coding in [Vibe vs Agentic](/getting-started/vibe-vs-agentic).
- **Simon Willison's blog** — One of the most consistent, hype-free chroniclers of practical
  LLM and AI-coding technique. [simonwillison.net](https://simonwillison.net)
